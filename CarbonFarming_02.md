
Carbon farming is a set of agricultural practices and strategies aimed at reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions and enhancing carbon sequestration in soils and landscapes. It involves land management techniques that help mitigate climate change by capturing and storing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, while also providing various environmental and economic co-benefits. In the European Union (EU), carbon farming is increasingly recognized as a key component of climate action policies, particularly within the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) framework.

### Key Components of Carbon Farming

1. **Definition and Approach:**
   - Carbon farming can be implemented through two main approaches: action-based and result-based payments. Action-based schemes pay farmers for adopting specific practices, such as agroforestry or organic farming, that are expected to reduce emissions or sequester carbon. Result-based schemes, on the other hand, tie payments directly to measured outcomes, such as verified reductions in GHG emissions or increases in soil carbon stocks ([Source: T14_IPOL_STU(2021)695482_EN, Page 10]).
