Soil health plays a crucial role in influencing human health in several interconnected ways:

1.	Nutritional Quality of Food: Healthy soils produce safe and nutritious food. The soil nutrient content of crops is directly linked to soil health, which affects human nutrition and overall health. Poor soil health can lead to nutrient-deficient crops, impacting dietary quality.
2.	Water Quality: Healthy soils act as natural filters, helping to purify water by trapping contaminants and preventing them from reaching groundwater supplies. Contaminated water can pose serious health risks, including gastrointestinal diseases and other health issues.
3.	Microbial Exposure: Exposure to a diverse and healthy soil microbial community can benefit human health by supporting the immune system. This exposure may help reduce the risk of certain diseases and allergies, particularly in children, by promoting a balanced immune response.
4.	Ecosystem Services: Soil degradation negatively impacts soil ecosystem services, such as soil carbon storage, water regulation, and soil biodiversity. These services are essential for maintaining a healthy environment, which in turn supports human health. For example, degraded soils can lead to increased flooding and heat islands, affecting urban populations' health and well-being.
5.	Contaminant Risks: Soil contamination from industrial activities or agricultural chemicals can harm human health through various exposure routes, including ingestion, inhalation, or skin contact. This can lead to chronic health issues and increased vulnerability, especially in sensitive populations like children and the elderly.
6.	Mental and Physical Well-being: Access to green spaces and healthy ecosystems, supported by healthy soils, is vital for mental and physical well-being. Urban areas with healthy soils can provide recreational spaces that enhance quality of life and reduce stress.
7.	Climate Resilience: Healthy soils contribute to resilience against climate-related extremes, such as droughts and floods. Improved soil health can mitigate the economic and health impacts associated with these events, reducing casualties and financial losses.
